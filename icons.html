THE ICONS

The voices that defined Zimbabwe’s sound in the 80s & 90s

The Nzou of Music

Oliver Mtukudzi (1952–2019)

Afropop icon known for Tuku Music — a blend of mbira-inspired guitar, traditional rhythms, and soulful vocals. His songs often tackled social issues like HIV/AIDS, responsibility, and dignity, shaping conversations across communities.

Key works Neria · Todii · Bvuma/Tolerance

The King of Sungura

Alick Macheso (b. 1968)

Sungura bandleader famed for intricate bass lines and high-tempo guitar interplay. His music became the sound of working-class celebration, uniting mineworkers, vendors, and township audiences at shows and on radio.

Key works Simbaradzo · Zvakanaka Zvakadaro · Vapupuri Pupurai

~30s sample

Gospel Pioneer

Charles Charamba (b. 1971)

Gospel artist whose Scripture-rooted lyrics and call-and-response choruses brought church sounds to the mainstream, strengthening community identity and inspiring youth choirs nationwide.

Key works Ivai Neni · Machira Chete · Exodus
