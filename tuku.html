Tuku’s Legacy

Celebrating Oliver “Tuku” Mtukudzi’s life, music, and enduring impact

“Todii” — Backstory and Social Impact

“Todii” asks, “What shall we do?” and became a compassionate call during Zimbabwe’s HIV/AIDS crisis. Through empathetic lyrics and restrained instrumentation, the song challenged stigma, promoted dignity, and gave voice to families caring for the ill. Its message travelled beyond borders and anchored Tuku’s role as a socially conscious artist.

Tiny Desk Performance

Oliver Mtukudzi’s NPR Tiny Desk set introduced Tuku Music to a global audience in an intimate format. The session includes “Todii,” showcasing his storytelling, guitar work, and ensemble dynamics.

Career Milestones

  • 1994 — First International Tour:

    Europe (Austria & Switzerland). Also toured South Africa with Lucky Dube.

  • 1998 — First International Collaboration:

    Worked with Bonnie Raitt (USA) on “One Belief Away,” inspired by Tuku’s “What’s Going On?”

  • 1992–2014 — Major Awards:

    M-Net Best Soundtrack (Neria)
    KORA Awards
    Honorary Degree (University of Zimbabwe)
    Global Service Award (USA)

  • 2019 — National Hero Recognition:

    Declared Zimbabwe’s first musician to receive National Hero status.
